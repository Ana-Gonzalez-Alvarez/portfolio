Technical profilePerfil técnico

I am a Computer Science Honors student at Miami Dade College (Padron Campus) with a focus on cybersecurity and systems security. My academic interests include post-quantum cryptography, secure system design, and the performance tradeoffs involved in implementing cryptographic mechanisms in real environments. I previously contributed to cybersecurity research under the mentorship of Dr. Selcuk Uluagac at Florida International University. In that role, I supported comparative analysis between post-quantum and traditional cryptographic systems, participating in technical evaluation and documentation of results. This work strengthened my interest in the intersection of mathematical theory, computational complexity, and secure implementation. In parallel, I participated in the development of an autonomous robotics project presented at AGU 2025, focused on sustainability and technical outreach. I also served in academic support and student leadership roles, reinforcing my commitment to technical rigor, structured problem solving, and collaborative environments. I am currently focused on deepening my foundation in secure systems and preparing for advanced study in cybersecurity.
